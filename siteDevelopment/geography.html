Gold in the Gulf

Dubai is one of the seven Emirates and the business capital of United Arab Emirates, a country situated on the Persian Gulf in the Western Asia, bordering Oman and Saudi Arabia. The city uses Gulf Standard Time – GST, which is GMT/UTC +4 hrs. Dubai lies directly within the Arabian Desert with its bordering Emirates, to the south – Abu Dhabi, the Emirate’s capital city and Sharjah to the northeast border.

Dubai is the biggest city besides the country’s capital – Abu Dhabi. Despite having skyrocketing buildings in the downtown area, it mainly consists of fine golden sands, vast sandy deserts and a flat coastline at the east of the city. Rugged and undeveloped roads to rocky mountains are at present in the farther eastern section of the city. Living in Dubai has its pros and cons, being a city located in the middle east, expect a climate with a scorching summer season peaking up to 46 degrees. Dubai is considered hot and arid throughout the year.
