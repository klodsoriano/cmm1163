A trade gateway to the world

Thriving hub for global trade

With its strategic geographic positioning and advanced logistics and infrastructure, Dubai acts as a global hub for trade.

A Concrete Jungle

The economy in Dubai boomed only after the discovery of oil in the early 1960’s. Dubai expanded its economy mainly in trade, real estate, tourism and mega construction projects in addition to its oil industry. Today, Dubai is considered a great place for many international companies to have their headquarters located in. The city is situated right in the middle of the gulf, a perfect trading port between the western countries and Asia – a wealthy trading hub in the region..

Dubai is home to almost everything. Foreign workers flocked into the city drawn by the broad booming industry and being a tax-free country. What made Dubai a haven for expatriates is that you get to live and work in the city without getting taxed by the government. Expatriates moving to Dubai can easily lands a job in their profession. It could be in sales, leisure, construction or even in tourism. Dubai’s main goal is to be the world’s “first” in everything they do, by this they get the world’s attention – a great way to drive investors and the global market.
